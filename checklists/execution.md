# Execution Checklist

- The current slice is small enough to explain plainly.
- The intended outcome for this slice is written down.
- The seam or stopping point is known before coding starts.
- The verification method is known before coding starts.
- Work is proceeding in small reversible changes.
- Notes reflect reality, not yesterday's plan.
- Unknowns are being turned into explicit questions.
- Broad speculative cleanup is being deferred unless it blocks progress.
- Each meaningful change is followed by a check.
- The next step is clear before stopping.
